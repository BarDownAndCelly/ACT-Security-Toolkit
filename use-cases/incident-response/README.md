# Incident Response

Incident response is a race against time. Security teams must quickly determine what happened, assess business impact, contain threats, preserve evidence, and restore normal operations—often while working with incomplete information.

AI can assist responders by rapidly organizing large volumes of security telemetry, summarizing investigative findings, identifying attack patterns, and generating structured documentation. While AI can significantly reduce repetitive work, investigators remain responsible for validating evidence, making response decisions, and maintaining investigative integrity.

## How AI Can Help

AI can assist incident responders by:

* Accelerating alert triage and investigation
* Summarizing logs, telemetry, and forensic evidence
* Identifying indicators of compromise (IOCs)
* Correlating activity across multiple data sources
* Recommending containment and recovery actions
* Generating investigation summaries and reports
* Translating technical findings for executives and non-technical stakeholders

---

## Investigation Use Cases

### Account Compromise Investigation

Analyze authentication activity, account misuse indicators, and unauthorized access patterns to determine the likelihood and scope of compromise.

### Cloud Account Compromise Investigation

Investigate suspicious activity involving Microsoft 365, Entra ID, Azure resources, and cloud administration actions to identify unauthorized access and assess potential business impact.

### Business Email Compromise (BEC)

Investigate executive impersonation, payment fraud attempts, mailbox compromise, and malicious email activity to assess risk and recommend response actions.

### Malware Triage Investigation

Review endpoint activity, process execution, network communications, persistence mechanisms, and security alerts to assess potential malware infections.

### Ransomware Investigation

Analyze ransomware-related activity, identify indicators of compromise, assess business impact, and recommend containment and recovery actions.

## Why AI Helps

Incident responders are often required to analyze thousands of alerts, logs, emails, and endpoint events while simultaneously coordinating response efforts and communicating with stakeholders.

AI can dramatically reduce the time spent summarizing evidence, organizing findings, generating documentation, and identifying investigative leads. This allows responders to focus on validating evidence, containing threats, making informed decisions, and restoring business operations.

---

## Core Principle

AI can accelerate incident response by improving efficiency and consistency, but responders remain responsible for validating evidence, preserving investigative integrity, maintaining chain of custody where applicable, and making all containment and recovery decisions.
