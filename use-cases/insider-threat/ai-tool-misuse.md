# AI Tool Misuse Investigation

## Objective

Use AI to assist insider threat investigators in identifying potential misuse of enterprise and public AI platforms that may result in the exposure of sensitive, proprietary, or regulated information.

## Scenario

Security leadership receives alerts indicating that an employee may have submitted proprietary company information to generative AI platforms. Investigators must determine whether the activity represents legitimate business use, policy violations, or potential insider risk.

## Prompt

You are a Senior Insider Threat Investigator.

Review the following activity and determine whether it indicates inappropriate use of AI tools or potential exposure of sensitive information.

Employee:

John Smith

Role:

Software Engineer

Observed Activity:

* Employee accessed multiple public AI platforms during business hours
* Large volumes of source code were copied from internal repositories
* Clipboard monitoring identified transfers of code snippets into browser sessions
* Sensitive project names appeared within browser activity associated with AI tools
* Multiple prompts contained references to internal systems and architecture
* Employee had recently completed mandatory AI usage awareness training
* No approved business justification was documented for the activity

Provide:

1. Executive Summary
2. Risk Rating
3. Indicators of AI Tool Misuse
4. Alternative Explanations
5. Recommended Investigative Actions
6. Potential Business Risks

## Example Output

### Executive Summary

The activity contains indicators consistent with potential misuse of AI platforms involving proprietary company information. Further investigation is required to determine whether sensitive information was exposed and whether policy violations occurred.

### Risk Rating

High

### Indicators of AI Tool Misuse

* Transfer of source code into AI platforms
* References to internal systems and architecture
* Use of unapproved AI services
* Lack of documented business justification
* Potential exposure of proprietary information

### Alternative Explanations

* Approved development assistance workflows
* Authorized testing activities
* Use of approved enterprise AI platforms
* Misclassification of transferred content

### Recommended Investigative Actions

* Review AI platform access logs
* Assess the sensitivity of submitted content
* Validate compliance with AI governance policies
* Interview employee and manager
* Determine whether data retention or model training risks exist
* Coordinate with Legal, Compliance, and Security teams

### Potential Business Risks

* Intellectual property exposure
* Source code disclosure
* Regulatory violations
* Confidential information leakage
* Contractual compliance issues
* Reputational damage

## Why AI Helps

AI can rapidly analyze user activity, identify potential policy violations, correlate sensitive data exposure indicators, and generate structured investigative assessments that support insider threat, compliance, and data protection teams.
