# Board Risk Summary

## Objective

Use AI to transform technical security findings into concise, business-focused risk summaries suitable for board members and executive leadership.

## Scenario

Security leadership must brief the Board of Directors on significant cybersecurity risks, recent incidents, and emerging threats without overwhelming non-technical stakeholders.

## Prompt

You are a Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) preparing a quarterly board briefing.

Review the following information and create a board-level risk summary.

Security Events This Quarter:

* One ransomware incident contained successfully
* Three business email compromise attempts prevented
* Increased targeting by remote worker fraud schemes
* Critical vulnerability identified and remediated
* No confirmed customer data exposure

Provide:

1. Executive Summary
2. Top Risks
3. Business Impact
4. Risk Trends
5. Recommended Leadership Actions

## Example Output

### Executive Summary

The organization experienced several significant cybersecurity events during the quarter, including a ransomware incident and multiple fraud attempts. Existing controls successfully prevented material business impact and no customer data exposure was identified.

### Top Risks

* Ransomware
* Business Email Compromise
* Remote Worker Fraud
* Third-Party Risk

### Business Impact

Operational disruption was limited and no material customer impact occurred. Continued investment in detection, response, and employee awareness remains important.

### Risk Trends

Identity-based attacks and fraud-related activity continue to increase across the threat landscape.

### Recommended Leadership Actions

* Continue cybersecurity investment
* Support employee awareness initiatives
* Review business continuity capabilities
* Monitor emerging fraud threats

## Why AI Helps

AI can quickly transform technical findings into concise, board-ready language that focuses on risk, business impact, and strategic decision-making.
